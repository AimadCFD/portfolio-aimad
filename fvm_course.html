1. Introduction to FVM

The Finite Volume Method (FVM) is a widely used technique for solving partial differential equations, especially in fluid dynamics and heat transfer. FVM works by dividing the computational domain into control volumes, ensuring the conservation of quantities (such as mass, energy, or momentum) at the control volume level.

Why use FVM?

  • It ensures conservation laws are respected.
  • Flexible for complex geometries and unstructured grids.
  • Widely used in Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) and heat transfer problems.

3. Discretization in FVM

To apply FVM, the continuous governing equations are discretized. The process involves dividing the domain into control volumes (CVs) and integrating the governing equations over each control volume.

Key steps in discretization:

  • Divide the domain into control volumes.
  • Apply the divergence theorem to convert volume integrals to surface integrals.
  • Discretize the fluxes through the control volume faces.
